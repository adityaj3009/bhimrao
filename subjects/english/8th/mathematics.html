Chapter 1: Rational Number & Irrational Number

About This Chapter

Understand number types, decimal forms, properties, and real number classification.

  • Rational numbers are fractions or decimals.
  • Irrational numbers have non-repeating decimals.
  • Both are part of real numbers.
  • Helps study organisms systematically

Chapter 2: Angle Pairs and Transversal

About This Chapter

Identify angle pairs formed by a transversal crossing parallel straight lines.

  • Corresponding angles are always equal.
  • Alternate interior angles are equal too.
  • Co-interior angles sum to 180°.
  • Transversal intersects two parallel lines.

Chapter 3: Indices & Cube Root

About This Chapter

Learn how indices and cube roots simplify calculations.

  • aⁿ × aᵐ = aⁿ⁺ᵐ
  • (aⁿ)ᵐ = aⁿᵐ
  • Cube root is power of 1/3.
  • ∛27 = 3, since 3³ = 27.

Chapter 4: Altitudes & Median Of Triangle

About This Chapter

Understand triangle's altitudes, medians, and their unique properties clearly.

  • Altitude is perpendicular from vertex.
  • Median joins vertex to midpoint.
  • All altitudes meet at orthocenter.
  • All medians meet at centroid.

Chapter 5: Expansion Formulae

About This Chapter

Learn key algebraic identities for expanding expressions easily and quickly.

  • (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
  • (a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b²
  • a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)

Chapter 6: Factorization Of Algebraic Expressions

About This Chapter

Break algebraic expressions into simpler factors using common identities.

  • Take out common factors first
  • Use identities like a² - b²
  • Group terms to factor easily
  • Simplify complex expressions step-by-step

Chapter 7: Variation

About This Chapter

Understand how quantities change together in direct or inverse ways.

  • Direct variation: both increase together
  • Inverse variation: one increases, other decreases
  • Use constant to form equations
  • Graphs show type of variation

Chapter 8: Quadrilateral Construction & Types

About This Chapter

Learn types of quadrilaterals and how to construct them.

  • Quadrilateral has four straight sides.
  • Types include square, rhombus, trapezium.
  • Angles add up to 360 degrees.
  • Construct using given sides and angles.
