Chapter 1: OBSERVING SPACE - TELESCOPES

About This Chapter

Explore sophisticated astronomical observation techniques and high-precision telescope instrumentation.

  • Telescopes magnify distant space objects
  • Refracting and reflecting types exist
  • Space telescopes avoid atmospheric distortion
  • Used to study stars and galaxies

Chapter 2: STUDY OF SOUND

About This Chapter

Explore sound properties, wave behavior, vibrations, and acoustic applications deeply.

  • Sound travels as mechanical longitudinal waves
  • Produced by vibrations of objects
  • Requires medium to propagate through
  • Pitch and loudness define sound quality

Chapter 3: REFLECTION OF LIGHT

About This Chapter

Understand light behavior, mirror reflections, laws, and image formation principles.

  • Light reflects from smooth surfaces
  • Angle of incidence equals reflection
  • Plane mirrors form virtual images
  • Used in periscopes and devices

Chapter 4: CURRENT ELECTRICITY

About This Chapter

Study electric current, circuits, resistance, Ohms law, and applications.

  • Current flows through closed circuits
  • Ohms law relates voltage and current
  • Resistance opposes current in wires
  • Measured using ammeter and voltmeter

Chapter 5: WORK & ENERGY

About This Chapter

Understand work, energy, power, conservation laws, and mechanical applications

  • Work is force causing displacement
  • Energy is capacity to do work
  • Kinetic and potential are energy types
  • Power measures work done per second

Chapter 6: LAWS OF MOTION

About This Chapter

Explore Newton's laws, motion principles, inertia, force, and acceleration.

  • Objects stay still or in motion
  • Force equals mass times acceleration
  • Every action has equal reaction
  • Inertia resists change in motion
